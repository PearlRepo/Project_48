River Pollution

Plastic pollution is an emerging environmental risk due to its negative impact on ecosystem health and human livelihood.
Land-based plastic waste, carried to the sea through rivers, is considered a main source of marine plastic pollution.
Only a small fraction of plastics that are found in the terrestrial and aquatic compartments of river systems are emitted, and the vast majority can be retained for years, decades, and potentially centuries.
